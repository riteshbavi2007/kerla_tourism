Backwaters of Kerala

A timeless journey through canals, lakes and village life 🌿

Alappuzha Backwaters

directions_boat Houseboats water Vembanad Lake

Alappuzha is the most iconic backwater destination in Kerala and is often referred to as the “Venice of the East.” The region is crisscrossed by an extensive network of canals, lagoons, and lakes that form the heart of Kerala’s backwater tourism.

Traditional houseboats, locally known as kettuvallams, glide slowly through the waterways, offering visitors a unique opportunity to experience village life along the banks. Coconut palms, paddy fields, and small hamlets create a serene and picturesque setting.

The backwaters are connected to the vast Vembanad Lake, the largest freshwater lake in Kerala, which also hosts the famous Nehru Trophy Snake Boat Race held annually.

Ashtamudi Lake, Kollam

eco Ramsar Wetland map Eight Channels

Ashtamudi Lake is the second-largest lake in Kerala and is named after its eight prominent channels that branch out like palm leaves. It serves as the gateway to the Kerala backwaters from the historic port town of Kollam.

The lake is surrounded by lush coconut groves, small islands, and traditional villages where activities such as clam harvesting and coir making are still practiced.

Recognized as a Ramsar Wetland of International Importance, Ashtamudi Lake supports a rich ecosystem and offers a calm, immersive backwater experience.

Kuttanad

agriculture Below Sea Level grass Paddy Fields

Kuttanad is a unique region in Kerala known as the “Rice Bowl of the State.” It is one of the very few places in the world where farming is carried out below sea level, protected by an elaborate system of bunds and canals.

The landscape of Kuttanad is characterized by endless green paddy fields, narrow waterways, and traditional villages accessible mainly by boats.

Traveling through Kuttanad offers a deep insight into Kerala’s agrarian lifestyle and showcases a harmonious relationship between nature and human ingenuity.

Kumbalangi

group Eco-Tourism Village pets Fishing Culture

Kumbalangi is India’s first officially declared eco-tourism village and is a model for sustainable tourism in Kerala. Located close to Kochi, the village is surrounded by backwaters, mangroves, and coconut groves.

Visitors can observe traditional fishing methods such as Chinese fishing nets, crab farming, and clam harvesting, which form the backbone of the local economy.

Kumbalangi offers an authentic village experience while promoting environmental conservation and community-based tourism.

Kavvayi Backwaters

forest Mangroves directions_boat Kayaking

Kavvayi Backwaters consist of a beautiful cluster of islands surrounded by rivers, canals, and creeks in northern Kerala. Unlike many popular backwater destinations, Kavvayi remains largely untouched by mass tourism.

The region is rich in mangrove forests and supports a diverse range of bird and aquatic species, making it an excellent destination for eco-tourism and nature exploration.

Kayaking, canoeing, and village walks are popular activities, offering visitors a peaceful and immersive experience close to nature.
