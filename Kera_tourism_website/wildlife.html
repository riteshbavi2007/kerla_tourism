Wildlife of Kerala

Explore the untouched forests of the Western Ghats 🌿

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

pets Elephants & Deer forest Nilgiri Biosphere

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary forms an important part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve and is one of the most biologically rich wildlife regions in Kerala.

The sanctuary is home to elephants, tigers, leopards, deer, wild dogs, and over 200 species of birds, making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts.

Jeep safaris through Muthanga and Tholpetty ranges offer visitors a chance to experience wildlife in its natural habitat.

Silent Valley National Park

eco Pristine Rainforest public Western Ghats

Silent Valley National Park is one of the last undisturbed tropical evergreen forests in India, located in Palakkad district.

The park is famous for its unique ecosystem, rare medicinal plants, and endangered species such as the lion-tailed macaque.

Entry is strictly regulated to preserve the ecosystem, offering visitors a rare opportunity to experience untouched wilderness.

Eravikulam National Park

landscape High Altitude Park pets Nilgiri Tahr

Eravikulam National Park is located in the high ranges of Idukki district and is best known as the natural habitat of the endangered Nilgiri Tahr.

Rolling grasslands, misty hills, and seasonal blooming of Neelakurinji flowers make this park visually spectacular.

The park also offers panoramic views of Anamudi Peak, the highest peak in South India.

Parambikulam Tiger Reserve

pets Tiger Reserve water Reservoirs

Parambikulam Tiger Reserve, located in Palakkad district, is one of the most well-protected wildlife areas in South India.

The reserve is known for its sustainable eco-tourism initiatives and is home to tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and diverse bird species.

Visitors can enjoy guided safaris, trekking, and stay programs inside the forest while learning about conservation efforts.
