Experience Kerala — Culture, Food & Traditions

Discover traditions, festivals, cuisine and activities that define Kerala.

Traditions

Kerala's traditions are woven from its long history — from temple rituals and Ayurveda to the welcoming hospitality of its people. Observe centuries-old rituals at local temples, take part in village customs, and learn about traditional fishing and coir-making along the backwaters.

Tip: Respect temple dress codes and ask before photographing ceremonies.

Food

Kerala cuisine is rich, coconut-forward, and aromatic. Must-try dishes include: Sadya (vegetarian banquet served on banana leaf), Appam & Stew, Puttu & Kadala Curry, and seafood specialities from the coast. Don't miss street snacks like banana fritters and spicy roasted fish.

Where to eat: Local toddy shops, homestays for authentic Sadya, and coastal fish markets.

Festivals

Kerala celebrates many vibrant festivals: Onam — a harvest festival with boat races, floral carpets and Sadya; Vishu — new year rituals; and temple festivals featuring elephant processions, percussion ensembles and colorful pageantry.

Arts & Crafts

Experience classical arts like Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and traditional Kerala percussion (Chenda Melam). Explore local crafts — handloom weaving, mural painting, and coir products — often available at cottage industry centres and markets.

Experiences & Activities

  • Houseboat cruises in Alleppey and Kumarakom
  • Ayurvedic treatments and wellness retreats
  • Backwater homestays and village walks
  • Birdwatching in Thattekkad and wildlife safaris in Periyar
  • Tea plantation tours in Munnar and spice garden visits

Many experiences can be booked seasonally — plan according to festival dates and monsoon cycles.
