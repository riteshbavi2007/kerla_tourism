Waterfalls of Kerala

Nature’s cascading wonders hidden in the Western Ghats 🌿

Athirappilly & Vazhachal Falls

waterfall_chart Tallest in Kerala forest Sholayar Forests

Athirappilly Falls, often called the “Niagara of India,” is the largest and most spectacular waterfall in Kerala. Located on the Chalakudy River, it plunges from a height of about 80 feet, creating a breathtaking sight especially during the monsoon season.

Just a few kilometers away lies Vazhachal Falls, surrounded by dense tropical forests. While less steep, Vazhachal offers a serene environment with continuous flowing cascades and mist-filled surroundings.

Together, these waterfalls form one of Kerala’s most iconic eco-tourism destinations, attracting nature lovers, photographers, and wildlife enthusiasts.

Thusharagiri Waterfalls

hiking Trekking Trails mountain_flag Western Ghats

Thusharagiri Waterfalls is a group of cascading waterfalls located in Kozhikode district, surrounded by forested hills and rocky terrain. The name “Thusharagiri” means “snow-capped mountains,” referring to the mist formed by the falling water.

The waterfalls originate from the Chalippuzha River and are divided into multiple stages, making the area ideal for trekking and adventure tourism.

The scenic trails, fresh mountain air, and untouched greenery make Thusharagiri a favorite destination for trekkers and eco-tourists.

Palaruvi Waterfalls

spa Medicinal Waters water Natural Pool

Palaruvi, meaning “Stream of Milk,” is one of the most picturesque waterfalls in Kerala. Located in Kollam district, it cascades down from a height of around 300 feet.

The waterfall is believed to have medicinal properties due to the herbs and roots present in the surrounding forest. Visitors often take a dip in the natural pool formed at the base.

Palaruvi is easily accessible and offers a refreshing experience, making it a popular destination for families and nature lovers.

Mankayam Ecotourism

eco Ecotourism Zone pets Wildlife

Mankayam is an emerging ecotourism destination located near Thiruvananthapuram, known for its series of waterfalls hidden deep within forested hills.

The area is rich in biodiversity and offers guided trekking experiences through dense forests, river crossings, and natural rock pools.

Mankayam focuses on sustainable tourism, allowing visitors to experience untouched nature while ensuring minimal environmental impact.
