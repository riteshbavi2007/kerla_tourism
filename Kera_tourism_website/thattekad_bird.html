About the Sanctuary

A Haven for
Ornithologists.

Described by the legendary Dr. Salim Ali as the "richest bird habitat in peninsular India," Thattekkad Bird Sanctuary is a lowland forest located between the branches of the Periyar River.

Established in 1983, this sanctuary spans 25 sq km and harbors a diverse population of rare and endemic species.

Thattekkad Bird Sanctuary

Thattekkad Bird Sanctuary, also known as the Salim Ali Bird Sanctuary, is a haven for bird watchers. It is home to a wide variety of bird species and offers a peaceful retreat into nature.

Bird Species

  • Malabar Grey Hornbill
  • Indian Pitta
  • Orange-headed Thrush
  • White-bellied Treepie

Activities

Enjoy bird watching, nature walks, and photography. The sanctuary is best explored with the help of a local guide.
