Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary

A peaceful haven on the banks of Vembanad Lake — a top birding spot in Kerala.

About the Sanctuary

Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary covers about 14 acres of freshwater marshland and is home to many resident and migratory birds. Peak season for migratory birds is from November to February, when species like Siberian storks, herons, kingfishers, and teals visit the lake and reedbeds.

Top species to spot
  • Black-headed Ibis
  • Whiskered Tern
  • Indian Cormorant
  • Herons and Egrets
  • Kingfishers
Best time to visit

November–February (migratory season). Early morning and late afternoon offer the best sightings.

Activities

  • Guided birdwatching boat trips on Vembanad Lake
  • Walking trails and viewpoint hides
  • Photography sessions and nature interpretation

Visitor Info

Location: Kumarakom, Kottayam district

Timings: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Entry: Nominal fee; contact local tourism office for group visits and birding tours.

How to reach

Nearest town: Kottayam (20 km). Nearest airport: Cochin International Airport (~75 km). Regular local transport and taxis available from Kottayam.
