Explore Munnar

Detailed guides, itineraries and practical tips to make the most of your Munnar visit.

Overview

Munnar is a high-altitude hill station famous for its tea plantations, misty valleys, and biodiversity. Ideal for trekking, wildlife, and scenic relaxation.

Top Attractions

  • Eravikulam National Park — Nilgiri Tahr and Neelakurinji blooms
  • Mattupetty Dam & Lake — boating and scenic views
  • Tea Museum — history of plantation culture
  • Top Station — panoramic sunrise point

Suggested Itineraries

1-Day (Quick)

Early morning at Eravikulam, tea estate walk, Mattupetty Dam in the afternoon, sunset at Top Station.

2–3 Days (Relaxed)

Add guided treks, local village visits, and an optional drive to Kolukkumalai for sunrise.

Activities

Trekking, birdwatching, tea estate tours, mountain biking, and camping under the stars.

Practical Info

  • Best time: September–May (peak: Nov–Feb)
  • Altitude: ~1,600–2,700 m — carry warm clothes
  • Nearest airports: Cochin (COK) — ~110 km

Sample Day Plan (Full Day)

  1. 06:00 — Sunrise at Top Station
  2. 09:00 — Breakfast and tea estate walk
  3. 12:30 — Lunch near Mattupetty
  4. 15:00 — Visit Tea Museum and local markets
Quick Tips
  • Carry binoculars for wildlife and birds
  • Book guided treks in advance
  • Respect local communities and trails
